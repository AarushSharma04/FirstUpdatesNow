Game Manual 0 V0.2.0

Game Manual Zero, a community-sourced guide for FTC, has released their newest iteration, which now includes a glossary, robot gallery, and award descriptions. The Game Manual Zero team has been working hard to improve the quality of both the manual and site. This community resource can be helpful to teams, veteran or rookies. There are various parts inside the manual that can help various members in FTC, such as the hardware, software, and "awards" components that can be found on their website.

XRC Ultimate Goal Tournament

There will be an Ultimate Goal XRC simulator tournament this Saturday September 19th. The Ultimate Goal game was released in the simulator earlier this week and the deadline to sign up for the tournament is tonight. It's going to be a good way to see how this game could be played throughout the season to help teams practice, even in a virtual environment.

Actual scoring zones in ultimate goal

An alumni of FTC team 7129 the Robo Raiders created an amazing diagram depicting the shooting positions for scoring the Rings. As you may know, there's a restriction of not being able to shoot the Rings more than 16 feet. The diagram up on the screen right now shows the ranges from which you can score the rings, assuming the shooter is at max height on your robot. Feel free to check out the details of how the diagram works in the comment section of that reddit post.

REV Hardware Client

The new REV Hardware Client allows a team to update the firmware on a control hub or expansion hub over USB, rather than WiFi. In addition, REV's new client allows teams to access both Blocks and OnBot JAVA directly from their client. This tool will definitely be useful for teams this upcoming season with further adoption of the Control Hub.

World Championship dates changed

On September 8th, FIRST announced that both the houston and detroit championships would be pushed back to later in the summer with Houston happening in Mid-July and Detroit happening in early august. This allows the competition season to be pushed back till June and some FRC programs have already announced that they will have events in June.

OpenCV Guide for detecting rings in the Starter Stack

There is a rather interesting software challenge this year during the autonomous period, where teams have to determine if there are 0, 1, or 4 rings in a stack and position the wobble goal in a certain zone accordingly. FTC team 9794 Wizards.exe made an amazing video on how programmers can go about programming this aspect of the challenge using computer vision to accurately detect which of the three possibilities are in play.

FIRST COVID Safety Story on Instagram

FIRST recently reshared a post from a team on their Instagram story in order to advertise their new guidelines for teams meeting throughout COVID. In the photo, there is at least 1 student not wearing a mask and there are people giving each other a high five. This is a complete violation of the FIRST safety guidelines for COVID-19, and teams should take a better stance to stay safe and practice proper social distancing rules.
